Geolocation RPG

Role: Research · Prototyping · Documentation

Overview

Geolocation RPG is a location-tracking game inspired by Dungeons and Dragons that encourages players to move through real-world spaces while engaging with narrative, choice, and character progression. The project explores how location-based mechanics and ethical storytelling can support physical, mental, and spiritual well-being.

Context & Goals

This project sits at the intersection of game design, user psychology, and media studies. The primary goal is to understand how geolocation mechanics and narrative structure can:

  • Encourage players to explore their surroundings in meaningful ways,
  • Motivate play through implicit rewards rather than explicit rewards, and
  • Support different dimensions of well-being through quests and story events.

Design questions guiding the project include how far we can push tabletop-style roleplaying and decision-making into a location-based format without overwhelming players or turning the experience into pure grind.

Core Design

The game is structured around a node-based map of locations, each tied to quests, encounters, or story beats. Players move between nodes in the real world and unlock:

  • Story encounters – spiritually enrichening choices and dialog,
  • Combat mechanics – enemy encounters that are fun and engaging,
  • Class and character progression – light RPG-style upgrades suitable for both casual and competitive players.

The system borrows from tabletop RPGs (D&D-style roles and narrative branching) while remaining lightweight enough to be used in everyday life.

Process

The current state of the project has gone through several phases:

  1. Concept & system sketching: defining the core loop, the structure of nodes, and how tabletop-style decision-making could map onto GPS-based movement.
  2. Narrative and quest design: outlining small scenarios that fit into daily movement patterns (campus, neighborhood, commute) rather than requiring special trips.
  3. Prototype documentation: building design documents and flow diagrams to support future digital implementations.
  4. Early playtesting plans: preparing small-scale tests to validate whether the structure feels engaging without being burdensome.

Tools & Responsibilities

My contributions to Geolocation RPG include:

  • Designing the core game loop and node/region structure,
  • Drafting quests, events, and reflective prompts for different location types,
  • Documenting rules, flows, and system behavior for future implementation,
  • Prototyping combat mechanics and node systems using Python, and
  • Defining an initial research plan for evaluating engagement and well-being outcomes.

Tools used include digital documentation and mapping tools for structuring regions and nodes, along with standard design and note-taking software for iteration.

Status & Next Steps

The project is currently in the documented prototype stage, with system rules, narrative beats, and node structures outlined. Next steps include:

  • Building a small digital or hybrid prototype to test with a limited group of players,
  • Collecting initial feedback on usability, motivation, and narrative clarity, and
  • Refining mechanics based on real-world play and movement patterns.
